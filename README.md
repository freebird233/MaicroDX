Version Differences
------
Currently, there are two versions of MaicroDX: 1.0 and 2.0. They each have their own pros and cons. You can choose based on your tablet model and fabrication capabilities.
<br>
<br>
__1.0__ : Suitable for 11-inch and smaller tablets. Moderate size and material usage. Modify the cutout dimensions in the Solidworks project file according to your tablet's dimensions. A 180mm print bed size is recommended.
<br>
(Note: You can also use this model for tablets larger than 11 inches, but you will need to use the scaling function, which will increase the workload slightly.)
<br>
<br>
__2.0__ : Universal for 11-inch to 13-inch tablets. Larger size and material usage. It uses screws and sliders within the model to adjust front/back, left/right, and up/down positions. A 256mm print bed size is recommended.
<br>
(Note: Due to size constraints, an 11-inch tablet using this model will not show the full top screen. You will need to enable compact mode and zoom the screen to 1.0x or above.)
<br>
<br>

If you decide to use the 2.0 version model, please [click here](https://github.com/freebird233/MaicroDX/blob/main/README_2.0en.md)
<br><br>
If you decide to use the 1.0 version model, please __continue reading below__

Getting Started
------
<br>

`-1` Download the model project files from the [release](https://github.com/freebird233/MaicroDX/releases) and modify them according to your device dimensions. (Consider forking and uploading your own work to help more people!) 
<br>
    The 1.0 file is the version limited to 180x180mm dimensions, meaning you can use smaller printers like Bambu A1 Mini! When used for an 11-inch tablet, set the game scale to about 85%.
    
 

<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
`0` Open "[New] Electronic Switches.SLDPRT". The parameters are located in the "mipad5" and "camera" sketches within the left feature tree. I have used the comment feature in the sketches to label the dimensions (length, height). For thickness (width), right-click the feature to adjust the parameters. Finally, export as STEP and re-cut and arrange for printing.
<br> 
Note: When using the scaling function, all tolerances will be scaled proportionally. Ensure the dimensions at the switch mounting areas remain their original size.

<img width="720"  alt="1756996362818" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/24f96f36-5d1b-4a57-8a78-6013c7cfef21" />

<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 

`1` Print all model files. 3 walls, 5% infill, 0.10mm layer height.


<img width="720" alt="3711083a9521bbc4f4f6488003fff1e" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ecc5b479-58e1-40e5-b402-3925117ec06d" />


After initially securing printed parts 2 and 3, apply a small amount of glue to the connection points.


<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 

`2` Remove the outer shell of the 9-key keyboard and take out the PCB board.<br> 
<img src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c978c0e6-fdb4-48b0-9297-3cd38f5da331"  width="400" > 
<br> The image above shows the result after using a soldering iron to remove the black switch sockets. The solder pads should be clearly visible. <br><br> 
<img width="400"  alt="80c00f655ec31978e7290bde7d4e652" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b342f600-640d-496a-a27f-6eb37281e73f" />    
<br> ↑ Alternatively, you can solder the wires directly to the switch sockets. Pass them through the drilled holes, then apply AB glue at the center to secure the wires and prevent them from breaking under strain.<br><br> 

Connect the PCB keyboard to a computer and download the software provided by the seller to program the key mappings.
Consider turning off the LED lights to save battery power.
<img width="450"  alt="80c00f655ec31978e7290bde7d4e652" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/68191312-93d4-4be2-b3b4-c0a97311866e" />    <img width="300"  alt="80c00f655ec31978e7290bde7d4e652" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/13a27dda-3d21-4a31-9b79-2fc683db114c" />   
<br> Configuration Software: https://www.jianguoyun.com/p/DVZ2nxEQ1raWDRiXhOcFIAA

<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 

`3` Insert the low-profile red switches into the 8 slots on the button wheel. The two pins of the switch should face the center of the wheel to be installed correctly.
<img width="480" alt="3711083a9521bbc4f4f6488003fff1e" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4b009ba8-dc1f-4dcd-bda6-332664c53bbc" /> <img width="310" alt="3711083a9521bbc4f4f6488003fff1e" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/80e9c5f5-3ccb-4673-8051-260165546d74" />

<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 

`4` Solder wires to the contact points of each button. Measure the length to ensure they can pass through the stand's headphone hole and connect to the main board on the back. It is recommended to route each button individually to ensure the buttons correspond exactly to the programmed key mappings.
To prevent accidental screen touches after installation, cover the bottom of each button with electrical tape.
<img width="480" alt="3711083a9521bbc4f4f6488003fff1e" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/f6c62ac2-c91a-4544-923b-08250b3d07d5" /> <img width="480" alt="3711083a9521bbc4f4f6488003fff1e" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/de28197e-5f19-4377-9f73-a84c55812848" />

A total of 16 wires. Slowly arrange them into the side slot of the headphone hole.
(This is the most difficult part of the project due to size constraints, leaving very little room for routing. Please be patient and take your time.)



<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 


`5` Try carefully inserting the tablet into the stand.
If there are protruding particles or support fragments, use a file to sand them down.
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 

`6` Place the OTG right-angle adapter at the bottom of the tablet. A clearance of about 1.5mm was reserved during the design. Consider using nano tape to raise it slightly to ensure it fully plugs into the tablet. Once confirmed, secure it with glue (custom-made fixings are recommended).
Use the data cable provided by the PCB board seller to connect the OTG Type-A port to the keyboard's Type-C port. Check if the buttons are outputting correctly.

<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
<br> 
Finally, paint, secure, and enjoy it!
